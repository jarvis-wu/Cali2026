If you've never driven Highway 1 through Big Sur, put it on your list immediately. The road hugs the cliffs above the Pacific — one side is sheer rock face, the other is a 300-foot drop into crashing waves. It is absurd.

## The Drive

We left San Francisco at 6:30am and took the coastal route south. The Golden Gate Bridge in morning fog was surreal. After crossing into Marin, we picked up Highway 1 and didn't let go for the next four hours.

**Highlights:**
- Bixby Creek Bridge (worth stopping for photos — pull-outs on both sides)
- McWay Falls — a waterfall that drops directly onto the beach
- Elephant seal colony at Piedras Blancas — hundreds of them just lying around

## Where We Stayed

We grabbed a campsite at Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park — $35/night, reservations required months ahead. Worth every bit of planning.

<div class="tip">

**Pro tip:** The drive takes much longer than Google Maps suggests. Every overlook will tempt you to stop. Budget at least 5–6 hours for the full stretch.
