This is the one we planned the whole trip around. Half Dome is a 14–16 mile round trip with nearly 5,000 feet of elevation gain. The last 400 feet are straight up a granite face on steel cables. Permits are required and hard to get — we have ours.

Alarm goes off at 4am. On trail by 5.

## The Route

Happy Isles → Vernal Fall → Nevada Fall → Half Dome Junction → Sub Dome → the cables → summit. Same way back.

## Mile 0–3: Happy Isles to Vernal Fall

The trail starts in the dark. Headlamps on, single file up the Mist Trail. By the time we hit the top of Vernal Fall the sun will be rising over the valley. Stop here to refill water — last reliable source for a while.

**Vernal Fall** is 317 feet straight down. The trail goes right alongside it, close enough to get soaked.

## Mile 3–6: Nevada Fall and the Long Climb

Nevada Fall is bigger and quieter than Vernal — most day hikers turn back before this point. After the falls the trail opens into a high meadow. Half Dome comes into view for the first time and it looks impossibly far away.

This stretch is relentless. Just keep moving.

## Mile 6–8: Sub Dome

The Sub Dome is the staircase before the main event — 600 steep stone steps carved into the rock. Permit checkers are stationed here. Legs will be burning. Take the break, drink water, eat something.

From the top of the Sub Dome, the cables are visible. This is where it gets real.

## The Cables

The final 400 feet of Half Dome are climbed using two steel cables bolted into the granite at a 45° angle. Wooden rungs are spaced every 10 feet as footholds. Gloves are mandatory — the cables are metal and will shred bare hands. There are gloves in a pile at the base; take a pair.

Go slow. Don't look down more than you need to. One person at a time on each side.

<div class="tip">

**Do not attempt the cables if there's any sign of afternoon storms.** Lightning on exposed granite kills people every year. If clouds are building by 11am, turn around at the Sub Dome. The summit is not worth dying for.

</div>

## The Summit

The summit is a 13-acre granite plateau at 8,800 feet. On a clear day we can see 300 miles in every direction — the Central Valley, the Sierra Nevada spine, the Merced River far below. Sit down, eat everything left in the pack, and take it in for as long as we want.

We earned this.

## The Way Down

Going down takes longer than most people expect. Knees take the worst of it on the descent from Nevada Fall. Trekking poles help. Save something in the tank — it's still 8 miles back to Happy Isles.
